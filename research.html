Scarlet

Secure Campus Reporting

Scarlet was created over the course of one semester for an SI 582 project. It was an individual project where I took on the role of UX Researcher and UX Designer

The Process Behind Scarlet

Countless crimes against students occur daily on college campuses, but only a small percentage are ever reported. Even a smaller percentage are made known to the general student population. Although email notifications are in place for students to receive Crime Alerts, these alerts do not get sent out for every transgression that is reported, nor are Crime Alerts sent out for the majority of violent crimes (assault) that occur on campus.

The Solution

To address the lack of transparency regarding crimes committed on campus, I have designed a mobile application that allows users to input their University and view a heat map of crimes reported on campus, as well as submit a report. Users can sort through reports based on location or category.

Insights from User Research

In order to better understand the scope of this project, competitive analysis of similar resources and user interviews were conducted. Through my competitive analysis research, I found that similar applications really only focused on crisis notifications: using location services to contact friends, family or the police during an emergency. It seemed as though no application focused on informing people living around a college campus of unsafe areas and crimes reported to police, or campus officials. I could have included location tracking services for emergency situations, but I figured there are enough tools already out there that do this and the inclusion would have confused users on the main purpose of this application. Instead, I chose to focus on what interviewees wanted to see the most -- a constantly updating heat map depicting everything from commonplace crimes to violent crimes. This design allows users to stay informed and more up-to-date about activities occuring in their area.
